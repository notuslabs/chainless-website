FINTECH & CRYPTO NAVIGATION PATTERNS RESEARCH
================================================
Date: 2026-04-06
Scope: Top nav labels, section flow, grouping conventions
Sites analyzed: Nubank, Wise, N26, Revolut, Kraken, Ledger, Phantom, Exodus, Coinbase


1. ACTUAL NAV LABELS FOUND
--------------------------

Nubank:         Ultravioleta | Nu Empresas | Seguranca (Security)
                (3 items + CTA button)

Wise:           Personal | Business | Platform
                (3 items + Help, Login, Sign up)

N26:            Plans | Bank | Save | Invest | Travel
                (5 items)

Kraken:         Individuals | Businesses | VIP | Affiliates | Markets | About
                (6 items)

Ledger:         Products | Apps and Services | Learn | For Business | For Developers | Support
                (6 items)

Phantom:        Features | Learn | Explore | Company | Developers | Support
                (6 items)

Coinbase:       Explore | Individuals | Businesses | Developers | Company
                (5 items, confirmed via design system sidebar: Home, Assets, Trade, Pay, Earn, Borrow, DeFi)


2. NAV ITEM COUNT: INDUSTRY STANDARD
-------------------------------------

Range:      3-6 items (excluding Login/CTA)
Sweet spot: 4-5 items
Neobanks:   Lean toward fewer (3-5)
Crypto:     Lean toward more (5-6), often with mega-menus

Key: The CTA button ("Sign Up", "Get Started", "Download") is ALWAYS separate
from nav items, never counted as one.


3. LABEL STYLE: NOUN-BASED DOMINATES
--------------------------------------

NOUN-BASED (vast majority):
  Products, Features, Markets, Plans, Company, Support, Learn

VERB-BASED (rare in top nav, common in sub-nav or app nav):
  Explore (Coinbase, Phantom), Send money (Wise sub-nav)

BENEFIT-BASED (almost never in top nav):
  Only N26 approaches this with "Save", "Invest", "Travel" — but these
  are still essentially product category nouns

PATTERN: Top nav = nouns. Verbs/benefits reserved for sub-menus, in-app
navigation, or landing page sections.


4. PRODUCT FEATURE GROUPING
-----------------------------

TWO MODELS OBSERVED:

A) UMBRELLA MODEL (most common for multi-product platforms):
   Single "Products" or "Features" top-level item with mega-menu underneath.
   Used by: Ledger, Phantom, Coinbase, Wise
   Sub-items then split into: Earn / Spend / Trade / Borrow / etc.

B) FLAT MODEL (used when product categories ARE the nav):
   Each product vertical gets its own top-level label.
   Used by: N26 (Bank | Save | Invest | Travel)
   Only works when categories are <=5 and crystal clear.

C) AUDIENCE MODEL (segments by user type, not feature):
   Individuals | Businesses | Developers
   Used by: Kraken, Coinbase, Wise
   Product features live inside each audience segment.

FOR CHAINLESS (4 distinct verticals: yield, card, credit, security):
- Umbrella model under "Products" risks hiding the value prop
- Flat model (Yield | Card | Credit) is viable since there are only 3-4
- N26's approach is the closest analog: direct product-category labels


5. LANDING PAGE SECTION FLOW (INDUSTRY CONSENSUS)
---------------------------------------------------

Canonical order from high-converting fintech pages:

1. Hero (headline + value prop + CTA)
2. Trust/Proof bar (logos, stats, regulatory badges)
3. Product overview / Philosophy (what makes this different)
4. Feature sections (individual product verticals)
5. How It Works (onboarding steps — high activation correlation)
6. Social proof (testimonials, case studies)
7. Final CTA (conversion section)
8. Footer (compliance, links)

SECURITY PLACEMENT:
- Security is almost NEVER a standalone top-nav item
- It appears as: (a) a landing page section between features and social proof,
  (b) a trust badge bar near the hero, or (c) a sub-page under "Learn" or "Company"
- Exception: Nubank puts "Seguranca" in top nav — but Nubank is a bank,
  not a crypto product. For crypto/self-custody, security is woven into
  the narrative rather than siloed.

"HOW IT WORKS":
- Never in top nav. Always a landing page section.
- Positioned after features, before social proof.
- Typically 3-step process with icons.


6. WHAT THIS MEANS FOR CHAINLESS
----------------------------------

CURRENT CHAINLESS NAV:
  Sobre | Rendimentos | Cartao | Como funciona
  (4 items, Portuguese)

RECOMMENDATIONS BASED ON PATTERNS:

Nav structure (4-5 items):
  Option A (N26-style flat):   Yield | Card | Credit | Security
  Option B (hybrid):           Products | Security | How it works | Company
  Option C (benefit-anchored): Earn | Spend | Borrow | Security

Key observations:
- "Sobre" (About) is rarely a nav item on product landing pages — it belongs
  in footer or under "Company". Landing pages assume the whole page IS the about.
- "Como funciona" (How it works) is a section anchor, not a nav destination
  on most sites. But for a single-page landing, anchor links are fine.
- "Security" as a nav item is unusual for crypto — BUT for a self-custody
  platform where security IS the differentiator, promoting it to nav-level
  follows Nubank's playbook of leading with trust.
- "Rendimentos" and "Cartao" map well to the N26 flat model.

The strongest pattern for Chainless specifically:
  Yield | Card | Credit | Security  (with How it Works as scroll section)

This follows N26's direct-category approach, keeps it to 4 items,
and elevates security — which is Chainless's key differentiator
against both CEXs (not your keys) and raw DeFi (too complex).
